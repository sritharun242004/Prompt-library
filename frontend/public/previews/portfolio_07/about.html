About

I am a front-end engineer based in Singapore with a particular obsession with CSS, motion design, and the craft of building things that feel as good as they look. I have spent the last eight years working on consumer-facing products at companies where performance on mid-range Android devices is not a nice-to-have — it is the product.

That constraint taught me things about the browser rendering pipeline that you do not learn building for high-end MacBooks. It also gave me a deep appreciation for CSS animation, because JavaScript animation is the first thing to degrade on a slow device. CSS running on the compositor is the only reliable path to 60fps on a 2019 Galaxy A-series.

This blog is where I write the tutorials I wish had existed when I was learning. Not quick tips that solve one problem and create three more — long-form explorations that give you a durable mental model you can apply to problems you have never seen before. When you understand the system, the syntax takes care of itself.

My favourite topics are CSS custom properties, animation timing functions, React hooks internals, and anything involving the browser rendering pipeline. I care about performance because I care about people — slow sites are an accessibility issue, not just an engineering metric. Start reading.
