Pichhwai

Nathdwara, Rajasthan · Vaishnava Temple Tradition, 17th Century

The Tradition

Pichhwai paintings are large devotional works created to hang behind the idol of Shrinathji in the Nathdwara temple. Each painting depicts a scene from Krishna's life in mineral pigments on cloth.

The Process

The cloth is treated with chalk and gum to create a smooth surface. Artists use mineral pigments mixed with gum arabic — no synthetic colours. Painting proceeds from background to foreground, with the figure of Krishna always last. A complex Pichhwai takes up to three months.
