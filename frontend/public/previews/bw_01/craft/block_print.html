Block Print

Bagru, Sanganer & Ajrakhpur · Ancient Indian Textile Tradition

The Tradition

Hand block printing is one of India's oldest textile traditions. Skilled craftsmen carve intricate patterns into wooden blocks, dip them in natural dye, and press them onto fabric in a rhythmic motion. Each stamp is slightly imperfect — a mark of the human hand.

The Process

Wood blocks are first carved by specialized block-carvers, sometimes taking weeks for a single complex design. The fabric is stretched on a printing table, the dye mixed to exact viscosity, and the printer works from left to right, aligning each stamp by eye. No two pieces are identical.
