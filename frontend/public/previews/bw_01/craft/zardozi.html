Zardozi

Lucknow, Agra & Delhi · Mughal Era, 16th Century

The Tradition

Zardozi is the art of embroidering with gold and silver metallic thread, often mixed with precious and semi-precious stones, sequins, and silk thread. The name derives from the Persian words zar (gold) and doz (embroidery).

The Process

The karigar stretches fabric tightly over a wooden frame called an adda. Using a hook-like needle called an ari, they pull the metallic thread through from below in tiny, precise movements. A skilled karigar can complete only a few square centimetres per hour. Complex bridal pieces require months of work.
