Bandhani

Kutch & Jodhpur · Ancient Resist-Dyeing Tradition

The Tradition

Bandhani is a resist-dyeing technique in which fabric is tied at thousands of tiny points before dyeing. When the ties are removed, each point reveals a small circle of undyed fabric, creating the characteristic visual language of dots.

The Process

Workers use their thumbnails — often protected by small thimbles — to pinch and tie each individual point with thread. A single piece may have 40,000 to 100,000 knots. After dyeing and untying, the dots appear as if lit from within.
