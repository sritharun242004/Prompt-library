Kalamkari

Sri Kalahasti, Andhra Pradesh · Ancient Painted Textile Tradition

The Tradition

Kalamkari — from kalam (pen) and kari (work) — is the art of hand-painting fabric with a bamboo pen. The Sri Kalahasti style is drawn entirely freehand using a tamarind-twig pen dipped in fermented jaggery and iron solution.

The Process

The fabric is treated with cow milk and myrobalan to make it mordant-ready. The artist then draws directly in iron-based ink. Colours from natural sources are applied in sequence, each requiring a drying period and wash. A complex piece takes up to three months.
