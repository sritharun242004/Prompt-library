Reader Questions, April Edition

On creative blocks, audience building, and whether to use a pen name

By Mara Ellison·Tuesday, April 9, 2024·9 min read

The question I get most often — in various forms — is about creative blocks. How do I start when starting feels impossible? How do I keep going when the middle is a swamp? How do I finish when the gap between what I imagined and what I have made feels unbridgeable? I have answered versions of this question many times, but I want to try a different answer here, one that I think is closer to true: creative blocks are usually information. They are telling you something about the project or about yourself that you are not yet ready to see.

On building an audience from zero: the advice I give most often is to write as if you already have the audience you want, not the one you have. This sounds like cope, but it is actually practical. When you write for a hypothetical reader who is smart and curious and already interested in the questions you are working on, you make different choices than when you write for a general audience or for no one. You use less scaffolding. You go faster to the interesting part. You trust the reader more. This produces better writing, which is the only reliable way to grow an audience.
