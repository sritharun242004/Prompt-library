Thread: What My Morning Actually Looks Like

No 5am wakeup, no cold plunge, just what actually works for me

By Mara Ellison·Sunday, February 18, 2024·6 min read

Thread on my actual morning routine — not the ideal version, but the real one, with all the boring parts included. I am sharing this because most morning routine content is aspirational rather than descriptive, and aspirational content is almost useless as a practical guide. Wake at 6:30. Make coffee. Read something physical for 20-30 minutes — recently working through a book about the history of cartography that has nothing to do with my writing. This is not productivity reading. It is the part of the morning that reminds me that interesting things exist outside my own concerns.

Sit down to write at 7:15 or so. I do not check email or any platform before this. This is the most important single decision in my morning practice, and it is the one that took the longest to make a genuine habit — about eight months before it became automatic rather than an act of will. The first thirty minutes of writing is mostly warm-up — I am writing into the problem, not yet solving it. I have learned not to delete the warm-up material immediately. Sometimes there is something useful in it. More often there is not, but the act of warming up is itself necessary.
