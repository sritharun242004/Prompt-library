About Thinking in Public

Thinking in Public is an independent newsletter about craft, creativity, attention, and the economics of intellectual work. It started in 2019 as a way to think out loud about questions I did not see being addressed honestly anywhere else — questions about what makes creative work worth doing, how to sustain a practice over years, and what it actually looks like to build a livelihood from independent writing.

Five years and 14,820 subscribers later, the newsletter has evolved but the core premise has not changed: this is a place for slow, careful thinking about hard questions. Nothing here is optimized for virality. Nothing here is written to a content calendar. Every issue exists because I have something worth saying that I could not say anywhere else.

The paid archive includes everything from detailed breakdowns of my business finances to frank discussions of client relationships, writing systems, and the specific tools and practices that have survived multi-year tests. It is the writing I do for people who are serious about the questions I am serious about.

Mission

The mission of this newsletter is simple: to write things that are still true and still useful five years after they are published. In a content environment optimized for recency and engagement, this is a genuinely countercultural bet. But I believe it is the only kind of writing worth doing — and the only kind of writing that builds a relationship with readers worth having.

Every piece I publish is written to this standard. If I cannot imagine it being worth reading in 2030, it does not go out. This makes the newsletter slow and irregular by design. It also means that the archive compounds in value rather than decaying — which is, I think, the most important property a body of work can have.

Who this is for

Writers and essayists

People who write seriously — or want to — and are looking for honest thinking about what sustains a writing practice over years, not months.

Independent creators

People building newsletters, podcasts, or other direct-to-audience creative businesses, who want the real numbers and the honest post-mortems.

Knowledge workers

People who think for a living — researchers, consultants, designers, strategists — and care about the quality of their attention and the depth of their thinking.

Thoughtful readers

People who read to think better, not to be entertained. People who want to engage with ideas that take more than thirty seconds to understand.

This newsletter is probably not for you if:

  • People looking for hot takes or reactive commentary
  • People who want a high-frequency content feed
  • People looking for beginner-level productivity tips
  • People who prefer short-form content
