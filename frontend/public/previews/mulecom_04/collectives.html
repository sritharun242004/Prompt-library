Chhattisgarh

Chhattisgarh Tribal Weavers

68% to artisan
Kondagaon, Chhattisgarh54 artisans148 livelihoods15 years active

Rooted in the Bastar region, these Gond and Halba women weave geometric patterns passed down over seventeen generations. The collective was formed in 2009 to preserve the endangered kosa silk traditions and ensure fair wages reach every weaver directly.

Gujarat

Gujarat Patola Heritage

72% to artisan
Patan, Gujarat38 artisans112 livelihoods12 years active

Patan Patola is one of the most technically demanding textiles on earth — each thread is resist-dyed before weaving so patterns emerge perfectly on both sides. This collective of Salvi family weavers has safeguarded the 900-year-old tradition and mentors a new generation of women weavers.

✓ GI Tag – Patan Patola✓ UNESCO Intangible Heritage Listed

Manipur Loin Loom

Odisha

Odisha Pattachitra

74% to artisan
Raghurajpur, Odisha42 artisans134 livelihoods10 years active

Raghurajpur is India's only heritage crafts village where every household is a studio. These 42 chitrakaras — including 19 women who traditionally stayed behind the scenes — now lead the collective and price their work based on hours, not buyer bargaining.

Tamil Nadu

Tamil Nadu Bell Metal

65% to artisan
Swamimalai, Tamil Nadu28 artisans76 livelihoods6 years active

Swamimalai's sthapathis have cast bronze for Chola temples for over 1,000 years using the lost-wax process. Today, 28 families — now including women for the first time in collective history — produce ritual objects and home decor, keeping an ancient metallurgical tradition alive and economically viable.
