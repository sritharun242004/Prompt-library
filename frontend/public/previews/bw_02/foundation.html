About the House

Foundation

DramaticLuxury was founded in 1998 in Kolkata with a single conviction: that Indian craft, properly understood, needs no embellishment — only a context equal to its depth.

The Beginning

A House Built on Cloth

The house began not with a business plan but with a bolt of Dhaka muslin purchased at a weaver's workshop in Murshidabad. The founder, standing in a room of looms older than the partition, understood that what she was looking at was not heritage — it was urgency.

Eight garments. A private showing in Kolkata. Twenty-three guests. That was the first collection. No press release. No campaign. Word moved the way it always has in this city — through conversation, through trust, through the garment itself being seen on a body that understood it.

Principles

The Archive

Memory as Medium

We do not design collections. We build an archive — a living record of the Indian textile tradition, restaged through a modern eye. Every piece we have ever made remains in the house record, studied, referenced, and occasionally revived.

The Craft

Making as Meaning

DramaticLuxury works exclusively with artisan communities across India. Kantha embroiderers from West Bengal, Jamdani weavers from Dhaka, zardozi ateliers in Lucknow. Their craft is not a supply chain — it is the source of everything.

The Client

Worn, Not Acquired

A DramaticLuxury garment is made to be worn. Not displayed, not invested in. The relationship between maker and wearer — mediated by the house — is the only transaction we believe in.

Chronicle

1998

House founded in Kolkata. First collection of eight handwoven pieces shown privately.

2001

First boutique opens on Park Street, Kolkata. Collaboration with kantha embroidery cooperative in Murshidabad begins.

2004

Mumbai atelier established in Kala Ghoda heritage building.

2009

Archive programme formalised — every garment produced receives a record number and material provenance document.

2013

Weaver residency programme launched: artisans spend two weeks each year at the Kolkata atelier in dialogue with the design team.

2016

Hyderabad boutique opens. Bidriwork collaboration with Nirmal craftspeople begins.

2019

Twenty-five year retrospective at the National Museum of Modern Art. Two hundred archived pieces exhibited.

2022

London Mayfair boutique opens. Archive digitisation project begins in partnership with the Crafts Council of India.

“Luxury is not the price of a thing. It is the depth of attention paid to it — by the maker, and eventually by the one who wears it.”

Founder, DramaticLuxury — Kolkata, 1998
