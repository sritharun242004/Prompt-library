Challenge

Terra's flagship Shoreditch store was opening in a market with four established specialty coffee destinations within 200 metres. Differentiation needed to be experiential, not just product-led — the product quality was already comparable across the category. Terra had a genuine story to tell about their sourcing relationships and farming partnerships, but no channel through which to tell it at the point of purchase. The brief was to create an experience that would justify a longer dwell time and generate the kind of earned media coverage that would signal cultural credibility to a London audience.

Approach

A 6-metre interactive wall maps the farm-to-cup journey in real time. Visitors can trace the coffee in their cup to the exact farm, elevation, and harvest week. The installation uses live weather data from partner farms in Ethiopia, Guatemala, and Colombia — so on a rainy Tuesday in London, you can see the dry season sunshine on the Yirgacheffe plateau where your beans were grown. We built a custom CMS for the installation that Terra's team can update without developer support, and designed a staff narrative training programme to complement the technology.

Outcome

Average dwell time of 22 minutes — 4× the category benchmark of 5.5 minutes. The installation was featured in Dezeen, Wallpaper*, and became the primary differentiator in earned media coverage that would otherwise have cost an estimated £340K in paid advertising. Instagram-originated footfall was tracked at 38% of new customers in the first three months.
