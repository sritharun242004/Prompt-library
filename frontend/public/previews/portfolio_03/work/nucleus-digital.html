Challenge

Nucleus's research dashboard was built by scientists, for scientists. When they needed to present findings to non-specialist boards and investors, the existing tools were inadequate — raw data exports pasted into PowerPoint, losing the nuance that made the research valuable. The Series A had been funded on the strength of the science. For the Series B, investors needed to understand not just what Nucleus had discovered, but what it meant for battery energy density, at scale, over time. The brief required us to build a communication layer without touching the underlying data infrastructure.

Approach

We built a presentation layer over the existing data infrastructure — no new database, no data migration. A React-based dashboard with configurable narrative templates that let researchers tell a data story without writing a single line of code. The visualisation library was built around the specific data types Nucleus works with: crystallographic structures, thermal stability curves, cycle-life graphs. Each visualisation type was designed with a non-specialist primary audience in mind, while preserving the ability to access underlying data for specialist review.

Outcome

Nucleus closed their Series B within four months of deployment, citing investor understanding of their technical progress as a key factor in due diligence. The dashboard is now used in every board presentation and was referenced three times by name in the Series B investor announcement. The visualisation library has since been adopted by two of Nucleus's academic research partners.
