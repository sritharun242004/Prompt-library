Challenge

Vantage Capital had outgrown their 2008 identity. The old mark signalled a different era — cautious, corporate, pre-digital. Operating across six asset classes and four continents, the firm needed an identity capable of carrying institutional gravitas while communicating the precision and forward-thinking approach that differentiates their investment philosophy. They needed to attract a younger LP base without alienating two decades of institutional trust. The existing typeface had become synonymous with a generation of firms that had since stagnated.

Approach

We started with the language before the logo. Three weeks of verbal identity work produced a tone-of-voice document that became the brief for visual identity. The new wordmark is geometric but warm — set in a custom-modified grotesque with a single curved terminal that softens the otherwise precise geometry. We built the full identity system around a grid derived from the proportions of a standard financial report, so that every application — from pitch decks to letter headers — has an inherent structural logic. The colour palette moved from the inherited navy-and-gold to a pair of near-black and warm white with a single accent.

Outcome

First new business pitch after launch closed within six weeks. The CFO called it "the first time we've looked as good as we think we are." Internal adoption exceeded expectations — the design system was implemented across 340 staff members in under a month, with a 96% compliance rate measured at 90-day review.
