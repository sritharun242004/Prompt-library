Challenge

Crest Outdoor was losing 68% of visitors at the product selection stage — not because of price resistance, but because of decision complexity. Their product range spans 2,400 SKUs across seven outdoor disciplines, and customers arriving from editorial content had high intent but low product knowledge. The existing site provided specification tables that meant nothing without context: a 900-fill power rating is useful information only if you know what temperature range that equates to in your intended use case. Crest needed a way to translate technical specification into genuine purchase confidence.

Approach

We built a context-first product discovery system. Before a customer reaches a product page, they define their use case — activity type, typical conditions, experience level, and priority (weight vs warmth vs durability vs price). The system then surfaces a curated shortlist with plain-language explanations of why each product fits their context, and where it falls short. The product comparison tool was rebuilt around decision criteria rather than raw specification. A trip planner feature — novel to the category — lets customers build a complete kit list for a planned trip, surfacing compatibility issues before purchase.

Outcome

Cart abandonment at the product selection stage fell from 68% to 31%. Average basket size increased 44% — attributed to the trip planner driving multi-item builds. Customer service contacts about product selection dropped 56%, recovering significant operational cost. The trip planner feature generated 28K user-created trip plans in the first 90 days and became the primary content marketing asset.
