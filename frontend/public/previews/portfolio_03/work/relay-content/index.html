Challenge

Relay wanted to launch a trade publication for the logistics industry that didn't read like every other trade publication in the logistics industry. Their core insight was acute: the people doing genuinely interesting work in logistics — the operators, engineers, and founders transforming supply chains — were not being served by the existing titles, which were pitched at procurement managers and focused on vendor announcements. The opportunity was to create a publication that those interesting people would actually read, not just receive. The challenge was that Relay had no editorial staff and needed both the strategic framework and the design system to be executable by a small team.

Approach

Editorial positioning first. We defined the reader persona, the content verticals, and the editorial voice before designing a single page. The result is a publication that reads like The Economist covering logistics — precise, analytical, without condescension. We designed a modular editorial system that allows a three-person team to produce a 60-page issue without external design support. The advertising inventory was structured around editorial context rather than position, commanding a premium that the standard logistics trade press cannot match.

Outcome

Launch issue reached 40,000 readers in the first two weeks — against a first-year target of 25,000. The advertising rate card sold out for the first six months within three weeks of the media kit going live. Second-year subscriber retention rate of 74% versus an industry average of 52%. Relay was acquired by a European media group 18 months after launch.
