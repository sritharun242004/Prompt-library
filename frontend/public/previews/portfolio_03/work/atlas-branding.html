Challenge

Atlas had won three major civic commissions in 18 months — a museum extension, a transport hub, and a university library. Their identity was 12 years old and designed for residential projects: warm, approachable, domestic in register. It actively worked against them in procurement contexts where their competitors were presenting with identities calibrated to institutional confidence. The rebrand needed to carry the weight of civic-scale work without sacrificing the collaborative reputation that had made them successful in the first place.

Approach

The new identity is built on a modular grid system that references architectural drawing conventions — the same system-thinking that governs their buildings governs their brand. The wordmark is set in a custom width-adjusted grotesque, with proportions derived from the grid. All applications — from site hoardings to proposal documents — use the same structural logic, so that every touchpoint feels like it belongs to the same disciplined mind. We built a website that leads with the civic portfolio and positions residential work as proof of range, not primary purpose.

Outcome

Shortlisted for two additional civic commissions within six months of launch — both of which they subsequently won. The identity was featured in Eye Magazine's annual identity review. Internal feedback from the team noted that the new identity had changed how they introduced themselves at networking events: with confidence rather than apology.
