The Health Insurance Reinvention

Niva Bupa, Star, and the insurtechs are rewriting what health cover means in India

Shreya Iyer·24 April 2026·11 min read

India's health insurance penetration sits at 37% — but that number obscures a deeper crisis. Most policies are bought once and never claimed. The companies rethinking the product from first principles are finding an entirely different market.

This analysis examines the structural forces behind the headline numbers, drawing on quarterly reports, regulatory filings, and interviews with industry insiders across India's major business centres. The reporting spans historical context, the current inflection point, and the scenarios that could unfold over the next twelve to twenty-four months.

Understanding this story requires going beyond the surface metrics. The real signal is in the unit economics, the competitive positioning, and the regulatory environment that will determine which players survive the next cycle. India's business landscape in Health is at an inflection point that rewards careful analysis over reactive commentary.

The data tells a counterintuitive story. What the market perceives as a straightforward narrative is complicated by on-the-ground realities that quarterly results rarely capture. This is why the conventional wisdom on this beat is systematically wrong — and why the investors, operators, and policymakers who get it right are operating from a different information set.
