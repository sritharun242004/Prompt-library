The AgriTech Last Mile Problem

Startups raised ₹8,000 crore to transform Indian farming. Farmers are still waiting.

Arjun Nair·22 March 2026·13 min read

AgroStar, DeHaat, Ninjacart — the agritech pitch was always about removing intermediaries and giving farmers direct market access. The intermediaries are still there. The startups have pivoted. Understanding why is a masterclass in the limits of platform thinking.

This analysis examines the structural forces behind the headline numbers, drawing on quarterly reports, regulatory filings, and interviews with industry insiders across India's major business centres. The reporting spans historical context, the current inflection point, and the scenarios that could unfold over the next twelve to twenty-four months.

Understanding this story requires going beyond the surface metrics. The real signal is in the unit economics, the competitive positioning, and the regulatory environment that will determine which players survive the next cycle. India's business landscape in Startups is at an inflection point that rewards careful analysis over reactive commentary.

The data tells a counterintuitive story. What the market perceives as a straightforward narrative is complicated by on-the-ground realities that quarterly results rarely capture. This is why the conventional wisdom on this beat is systematically wrong — and why the investors, operators, and policymakers who get it right are operating from a different information set.
