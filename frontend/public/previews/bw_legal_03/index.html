Got Questions?

Frequently Asked Questions

Everything you need to know about filing your ITR.

The due date for filing ITR for Assessment Year 2024–25 is 31 July 2024 for individuals not liable to audit. If you miss this date, you can still file a belated return up to 31 December 2024, but a late-filing fee of ₹5,000 (₹1,000 for income below ₹5 lakh) will apply.

ITR-1 (Sahaj) is for salaried individuals with income up to ₹50 lakh and one house property. ITR-2 is for those with capital gains or more than one property. ITR-3 is for business/professional income. ITR-4 (Sugam) is for presumptive income under Section 44AD/44ADA. Our wizard automatically selects the right form based on your income details.

The New Regime (default from FY 2023–24) offers lower tax rates but no deductions. The Old Regime allows deductions like 80C (₹1.5L), 80D, HRA, and home loan interest. Generally, if your total deductions exceed ₹3.75 lakh, the Old Regime is more beneficial. Our RefundEstimator calculates both regimes instantly so you can compare.

Tax refund is automatically processed by the Income Tax Department after you file your return and e-verify it. Refunds are typically credited to your pre-validated bank account within 20–45 days of processing. You can track your refund status on the ITD portal using your PAN and Assessment Year.

Key documents include: Form 16 (from employer), Bank interest certificates (Form 16A), AIS/26AS statement (auto-fetched), Investment proofs for 80C (PPF, ELSS, LIC), Health insurance premium receipts (80D), Home loan interest certificate, and Capital gains statements from brokers/mutual funds. Our platform auto-fetches Form 16 and AIS data.

Yes, you can file a Revised Return under Section 139(5) if you discover any mistake or omission after filing. A revised return can be filed any time before the end of the relevant Assessment Year or before completion of assessment, whichever is earlier. ClearFile Expert Help plans include 2 free revisions.

Form 26AS is your annual tax credit statement showing TDS deducted by employers and other deductors, advance tax paid, and self-assessment tax. AIS (Annual Information Statement) is a comprehensive statement of all financial transactions including property, mutual funds, and foreign remittances reported to the Income Tax Department. ClearFile auto-fetches both for you.

The CA prepares your return, suggests optimisations, and sends you a preview for approval. Once you approve, the CA files and e-verifies the return. You get a CA certification and ITR-V acknowledgement.
