Currently Working On

The Shadow Governors: How Private Equity Rewrote American Public Services

A book-length investigation into the acquisition of hospitals, prisons, water utilities, and public housing by private equity firms over the past two decades. The project spans 18 months of reporting, 200+ interviews with residents, former employees, regulators, and executives — and several thousand pages of internal documents obtained through litigation.

Supported by a grant from the Fund for Investigative Journalism. A companion series will run in The New York Times beginning autumn 2025.

If you have documents, tips, or direct experience with private equity in public services, contact via the secure form on the Contact page.
