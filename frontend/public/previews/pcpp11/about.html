About

Elena Vasquez

Investigative Reporter · Staff Writer, The New York Times

Elena Vasquez is a Pulitzer Prize–winning investigative journalist whose work has held governments, corporations, and institutions accountable for more than a decade. She is a staff writer at The New York Times, where she covers corruption, immigration, and the politics of power.

Her reporting has appeared in The New York Times, The Guardian, ProPublica, The Atlantic, The New Yorker, HuffPost, Politico, and Foreign Policy. She has investigated campaign finance dark money, private detention systems, pharmaceutical pricing, algorithmic criminal justice tools, and the privatisation of American public services.

She is the author of three books: The Paper Republic (Farrar, Straus and Giroux, 2019), longlisted for the National Book Award; The Price Equation (Penguin Press, 2021), winner of the Helen Bernstein Book Award; and Borders of the Mind (Riverhead Books, 2024), shortlisted for the Baillie Gifford Prize for Non-Fiction.

She holds a master's degree in journalism from Columbia University and a bachelor's in political science from UCLA. She has been a fellow at the Nieman Foundation for Journalism at Harvard and a visiting scholar at Columbia Law School's First Amendment Clinic. She is currently based in Washington, D.C.

Areas of Expertise

Investigative Accountability

FOIA litigation, document analysis, financial forensics, and long-term source development in closed institutional environments.

Immigration & Displacement

Reporting on immigration enforcement, asylum law, detention, and climate displacement across four continents.

Political Corruption

Following money through campaign finance, regulatory capture, and the revolving door between government and industry.

Healthcare & Pharma

Drug pricing, clinical trial data suppression, FDA regulatory failure, and the insurance industry's structural incentives.

Criminal Justice

Mass incarceration, private prison contracting, algorithmic sentencing, and public defender under-resourcing.

Data Journalism

Large-scale database analysis, cross-referencing public records, and visualization of systemic patterns at scale.
