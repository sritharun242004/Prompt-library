Between Tides

Koli fishing communities of the Mumbai coast

The Koli are the people who were in Bombay before Bombay was Bombay. They have fished the waters of the Mahim and Vasai creeks and the open sea off the Konkan coast for at least fifteen hundred years, and they were doing so when the Portuguese arrived in the sixteenth century, when the British arrived in the seventeenth, and when the textile mills transformed the city in the nineteenth. Each of these arrivals changed the Koli's situation; none of them displaced the Koli themselves, at least not entirely.

The Mumbai of 2019, however, is attempting something that the Portuguese and the British and the mill owners did not: it is attempting to be a global financial city, which is to say a city in which the space occupied by fishing villages, fish markets, and fish-drying grounds is economically irrational. The Koli are under pressure from coastal reclamation projects, real estate development, and the general logic of a city that has decided its waterfront is too valuable to be used for fishing.

I spent three months with Koli communities in Versova, Sassoon Docks, Worli Koliwada, and Malad. What I found was a community that was losing land faster than it was gaining rights — fighting individual development projects in court, negotiating with the Bombay High Court over the legality of coastal regulations, and continuing, in the meantime, to fish. The women who run the fish markets at Sassoon Docks and Dadar are formidable. They are not going anywhere without an argument.

The photographs in this series attempt to document a community in the middle of a long fight for survival in a city that would rather they weren't there. The fight is not dramatic; it is the steady, daily work of people who know their rights and are prepared to use them. What makes it worth photographing is not the drama but the ordinariness: the fish being sorted at 4am, the nets being mended on the beach, the boats going out before dawn. The ordinary persists even when the extraordinary is pressing in from all sides.
