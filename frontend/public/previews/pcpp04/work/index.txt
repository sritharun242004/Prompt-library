I photographed only in the evenings, from around four in the afternoon until dark, because the evening light in Old Delhi is unlike the light anywhere else I have photographed: it arrives at an angle that catches the ochre and red of the old buildings and turns the lanes briefly golden before everything goes dark and the sodium lamps take over.","What I was looking for was not the monument — not the Red Fort or the Jama Masjid, which have been photographed a million times — but the ordinary life that continues in their shadow. A week of visits produces photographs of surfaces. Two months in the evenings begins to produce something else — images from inside rather than from outside, made by someone who has, at least partially, learned to see what is being shown to them
