The Spice Journey

India grows more than seventy per cent of the world's spices. Our kitchen has spent four years tracking the best of them back to the source.

Ritu Dalmia·12 April 2025

It begins, always, with smell. The moment you open a bag of fresh Kashmiri deggi mirch — not the powdered, oxidised variety sold in most markets, but the whole dried pods from a small estate near Sopore — you understand immediately why the food tastes different here. We have spent four years building relationships with spice growers who sell almost exclusively to local markets, because they have no reason to scale and every reason to stay extraordinary.

Our masala blends are made in-house every 72 hours. The ratios are not secret — they are simply the result of many failed attempts and honest conversations with cooks much older than us. The vindaloo paste uses palm toddy vinegar sourced from a single family in Goa. The nihari masala includes a bone marrow extract that we render ourselves because the commercial version tastes of nothing.

If you want to replicate these dishes at home, we will teach you. Our spice workshop runs on the last Saturday of each month. Bring a notebook and do not wear anything you mind getting yellow.
