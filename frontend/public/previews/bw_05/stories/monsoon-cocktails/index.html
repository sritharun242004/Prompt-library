Monsoon Cocktails

The rain changes everything — the air, the produce, the mood. Our bar team builds an entirely new cocktail menu every July. Here is how they do it.

Priya Naidu·7 July 2025

The first monsoon rain smells of petrichor and possibility. It also signals the arrival of kokum, raw mango, and kairi — the sour green unripe fruits that appear in markets for exactly six weeks and then vanish. Our bar director Priya Naidu plans for these windows all year. By March she already has draft recipes waiting for July's produce.

The monsoon menu is built on restraint. In a city that has just been drenched and revived, the best cocktails are not elaborate — they are precise. This year's flagship is the Kanji Cooler: black carrot fermented with mustard seeds for five days, lengthened with sparkling water, garnished with a single dried red chilli. It takes the tongue from sour to sharp to warm in the time it takes to cross a puddle. Zero alcohol, maximum monsoon.

For those who want spirits, the Masala Mule swaps store-bought ginger beer for our own house brew, started every Sunday evening with fresh ginger, palm sugar, and a culture we've kept alive since we opened. The recipe changes slightly every batch. That is not a bug. That is the point.
