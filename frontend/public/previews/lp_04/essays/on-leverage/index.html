On Leverage and the New Economy

2024-01-15

Leverage is the force multiplier of effort. Every era has its dominant forms of leverage, and the people who understand and use the dominant leverage of their era are the ones who create the most value and capture the most of it. In the agricultural era, land was leverage. In the industrial era, capital and labor were leverage. In the current era, the dominant forms of leverage are code and media—products that can be replicated at zero marginal cost.

The extraordinary feature of code and media as leverage is that they are permissionless. You do not need anyone's approval to write software and publish it. You do not need anyone's approval to create content and distribute it. The old leverage—capital and labor—required permission from those who controlled it. The new leverage requires only skill and execution. This is a fundamental democratization of wealth creation, and it explains many of the unusual economic outcomes of the last twenty years.

Understanding leverage changes how you think about your time. Time spent building something that scales—software, writing, a YouTube channel, a systematic business process—is fundamentally different from time spent on activities that produce linearly. An hour spent building a tool that saves ten minutes a day generates value compounding in perpetuity. An hour spent doing the thing manually generates value exactly once. The mental habit of asking "could this be systematized, automated, or scaled?" is one of the most valuable you can develop.
