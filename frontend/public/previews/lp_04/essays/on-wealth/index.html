On Wealth: Assets, Not Income

2024-11-01

Wealth is not income. Income is the rate at which you earn money. Wealth is the stock of assets you have accumulated. This distinction matters enormously. A doctor earning five hundred thousand dollars a year who spends it all has no wealth. A teacher who saves diligently and invests for thirty years may be wealthy beyond what their income suggests. The path to financial freedom runs through ownership, not through earning.

The assets that generate wealth are those that produce value without requiring your direct time. A business, intellectual property, software, investments—these are the building blocks of real wealth. The fundamental insight is that your time is finite. Once you are renting your time for money, you have placed a ceiling on your wealth. The moment you own something that earns without you, the ceiling is removed.

Most people are taught to work hard, earn money, and spend it—perhaps saving a modest fraction. This is a prescription for a comfortable middle-class life, not for financial freedom. The wealthy understand that the goal is to accumulate assets as quickly as possible, live below your means during the accumulation phase, and then allow compound interest to do the rest. The window of aggressive accumulation is shorter than most people realize. Starting early is not optional—it is the entire game.
