Judgement Is Worth More Than Effort

2024-08-157 tweets

  1. Once you have enough leverage, judgement becomes the most important skill. With enough leverage, one correct decision can outweigh a thousand hours of hard work.

  2. Hard work is overrated. What matters is who you work with and what you work on.

  3. We evolved to survive in small tribes, not to make decisions about nuclear power plants and hedge funds. We have to train our judgement.

  4. The best way to improve judgement is to read broadly. Second best: learn from first principles rather than by analogy.

  5. When you have leverage, the quality of your decisions matters much more than the quantity of your effort.

  6. Collect mental models. Charlie Munger has a latticework of mental models. Build yours through voracious reading.

  7. The difference between the top 1% and the next 10% is often judgement, not intelligence or hard work.
