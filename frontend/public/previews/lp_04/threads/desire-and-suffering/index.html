Desire Is the Source of All Suffering

2024-06-016 tweets

  1. The Buddhists say desire is the source of suffering. The Stoics say it differently: suffering comes from wanting things outside your control.

  2. Every desire you have is a bet against your present happiness. You are saying: I will be happy when I get X.

  3. The antidote is not to want nothing. It is to want only what you already have, or what is truly within your control.

  4. You can train yourself out of most desires through substitution. Replace the desire for X with gratitude for what you already have.

  5. Notice when you are chasing an outcome versus enjoying a process. The happiest people love the process.

  6. The person who has few desires is the richest person in any room.
