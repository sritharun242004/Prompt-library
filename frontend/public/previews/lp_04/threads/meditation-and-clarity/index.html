Meditation: Observing the Mind Without Judgment

2024-06-227 tweets

  1. Meditation is not about emptying your mind. It is about observing your thoughts without being controlled by them.

  2. You are not your thoughts. You are the observer of your thoughts. Meditation teaches you this difference.

  3. The most important thing I have done for my mental health in the last decade is meditating every day.

  4. Start with five minutes. Sit quietly. When a thought arises, observe it and return to your breath. That is it.

  5. Meditation gives you a gap between stimulus and response. That gap is where your freedom lives.

  6. The modern world is designed to steal your attention. Meditation is how you take it back.

  7. After enough meditation practice, you begin to notice that most of your anxiety is about things that have not happened yet or things that have already passed.
