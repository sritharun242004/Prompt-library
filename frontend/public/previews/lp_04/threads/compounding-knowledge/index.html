Compound Knowledge Like You Compound Money

2024-02-206 tweets

  1. Knowledge compounds. Every book you read makes the next book easier and more meaningful.

  2. Consistency beats intensity. Reading one hour a day for a decade beats reading twenty hours a week for a month.

  3. The goal is not to memorize information. The goal is to build mental models that apply across situations.

  4. Cross-disciplinary knowledge is rare and valuable. Most people read only in their field. Read outside it.

  5. Teach what you learn. The act of explaining forces clarity. If you cannot explain it simply, you do not understand it.

  6. Your knowledge is the one asset that cannot be taken from you. Invest in it relentlessly.
