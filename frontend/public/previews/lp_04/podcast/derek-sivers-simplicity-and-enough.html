Simplicity, Enough, and the Examined Life

2023-11-0842mwith Derek Sivers
Show notes

Naval and Derek Sivers talk about voluntary simplicity, the concept of "enough," and what it means to design your life deliberately. One of the more personal and philosophical conversations in the archive.

00:03:45Naval

You have famously given away your company and chosen a very simple life. What led to that?

00:04:00Derek Sivers

I realized the things I thought I wanted—recognition, money, a big organization—were not actually giving me anything. The happiest I have ever been was when I had a small apartment, no employees, and worked alone on things I cared about. So I went back to that.

00:18:22Naval

How do you define "enough"?

00:18:35Derek Sivers

Enough is when you can stop and be happy with what you have without needing more. Most people never define it for themselves, so they are on a treadmill—always needing a little more. Define your enough, hit it, stop, and start living.

00:30:10Naval

What would you tell someone who is just starting out?

00:30:22Derek Sivers

Be very deliberate about what you want. Most people drift into careers, relationships, and lifestyles by accident and then wonder why they feel dissatisfied. Sit down, think hard about what you actually want, and then design your life around it. The examined life is much better.
