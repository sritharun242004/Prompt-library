Philosophy of Mind, Free Will, and Consciousness

Show notes

Naval and Lex Fridman go deep on some of the hardest questions: Is free will real? What is consciousness? Can AI ever be conscious? The conversation is wide-ranging—touching on Buddhism, quantum mechanics, and the limits of human understanding.

00:08:44Lex Fridman

Do you believe in free will?

00:08:47Naval

I believe in the feeling of free will, which I think is real in a practical sense. Whether it is metaphysically real—whether, given a complete description of the universe at time T, my decision at T+1 could be other than what it is—I genuinely do not know. Physics does not settle this. Neither does neuroscience.

00:22:13Lex Fridman

What is your current thinking on consciousness? Are you a materialist?

00:22:28Naval

I am agnostic. The hard problem of consciousness—why there is something it is like to be me—is genuinely unsolved. The materialist view is that consciousness is just what information processing feels like from the inside. The alternative views are stranger. I find the question fascinating precisely because I do not know the answer.

00:58:30Lex Fridman

Can artificial intelligence ever be conscious?

00:58:45Naval

If consciousness is substrate-independent—if what matters is the pattern of information processing, not the physical substrate—then yes, in principle. But we do not know if consciousness is substrate-independent. We do not even know what generates consciousness in biological systems. So we are asking whether we can replicate something we do not understand.
