Startups, Writing, and How to Think Clearly

2024-05-0158mwith Paul Graham
Show notes

Paul Graham joins Naval to discuss the relationship between writing and thinking, why startups are the best vehicle for wealth creation in the modern era, and how to identify what is genuinely important.

00:04:00Naval

You once said writing is thinking. What do you mean by that?

00:04:08Paul Graham

Most people think they have thoughts and then write them down. It is actually the reverse. The act of writing forces you to clarify your thinking. You cannot be vague in writing the way you can be vague in conversation. The discipline of putting ideas into sentences reveals whether you actually understand them.

00:18:45Naval

What do you look for in founders? The one thing.

00:18:52Paul Graham

Determination. Not intelligence—determination. The startup path is so hard, so full of unexpected setbacks, that the intelligent person who is not deeply committed will quit. The determined person who is somewhat less intelligent will persist and find a way.

00:38:22Naval

How do you identify what to work on?

00:38:30Paul Graham

Work on something that feels important to you and that you find genuinely interesting. The intersection of those two is rare. Most work is either important but boring or interesting but unimportant. The sweet spot is where they overlap.
