Building, Taste, and How to Move Fast

2023-09-251h 12mwith Patrick Collison
Show notes

Patrick Collison of Stripe and Naval explore what it means to build things of lasting quality, the role of taste in technology, and how Stripe manages to move fast while maintaining rigorous standards.

00:05:22Naval

Stripe is known for high standards and fast execution—two things most people say are in tension. How do you do both?

00:05:38Patrick Collison

I think the tension is real but overstated. High standards do not mean slow—they mean clear. When everyone on your team shares a deep understanding of what good looks like, you make decisions faster, not slower. The thing that slows you down is ambiguity about quality.

00:22:15Naval

You talk about taste. Can taste be developed, or is it innate?

00:22:28Patrick Collison

It can absolutely be developed. Taste is the internalized understanding of what quality looks like in a domain. You develop it by consuming a lot of the best work in that domain, by building things yourself and getting feedback, and by surrounding yourself with people who have excellent taste.

00:48:00Naval

What is the most important thing you have learned about building organizations?

00:48:15Patrick Collison

Hire people who are better than you in their area and then trust them. The founder who cannot let go of control creates a bottleneck that limits everything. The hardest skill in building organizations is knowing when to delegate completely and when to stay involved.
