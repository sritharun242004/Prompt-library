The Studio

Biography

MuseumDark is the practice of a photographer who began working in conflict zones and ended up in fashion, then found that both assignments asked the same fundamental question: what does it mean to be witnessed? The studio was formally established in 2015, though the work predates that by a decade — years of anonymous field work across Sub-Saharan Africa, South Asia, and the Middle East, accumulating an archive that would eventually demand a language commensurate with its weight.

The photographs resist easy categorization. A commercial campaign carries the moral seriousness of documentary. A fine art landscape holds the compositional logic of portraiture. This contamination is deliberate: the studio believes that genre distinctions are administrative conveniences, and that the most interesting work refuses them. The consistent thread is attention — a slow, patient, almost unreasonable degree of attention to the specific quality of light in a specific place at a specific moment, and to what that light does to the face or the landscape it falls upon.

The studio works with a small team of assistants and retouchers across New York and London, with regular residency periods in the field. Clients range from luxury fashion houses to international humanitarian organizations — the studio has no preferred client type, only preferred working conditions: time, access, and the freedom to bring a genuine point of view. Represented collections include the Tate Modern, the National Portrait Gallery London, and private holdings in fifteen countries.
