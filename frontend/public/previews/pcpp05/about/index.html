Who We Are

We believe photographs are the most faithful witnesses to a life well-loved.

StoryBookOpulence was founded in 2016 by photographer Aryan Mehta after a decade of editorial work across fashion and documentary. Witnessing how poorly lit and artlessly composed wedding photographs failed to capture the profound beauty of Indian weddings — some of the most visually spectacular celebrations on earth — he set out to build something different.

We are not a large agency. We are a tight, devoted collective of five specialists — photographers, cinematographers, and an album designer — who treat every wedding with the same level of creative attention we would give a commercial editorial shoot. The result is a body of work that has been featured in Vogue, Harper's Bazaar Bride, and India Today.

We work in India and internationally. We are obsessed with light. We love the moments between moments — the quiet breath before the pheras, the grandmother's tearful smile, the groom's nervous laugh. These are the frames that last forever.

The Team

Founder & Lead Photographer

Aryan Mehta

Aryan founded StoryBookOpulence in 2016 after a decade shooting for international editorial clients. His work is defined by a film-director's eye for composition and a documentary photographer's instinct for the unrepeatable moment. He has photographed weddings in 14 countries and across every major Indian destination.

Creative Director & Photographer

Priya Kapoor

Priya's background in fine-art photography and colour theory gives every StoryBookOpulence collection its painterly warmth. She leads all cinematic film productions and manages the creative post-production process to ensure every gallery feels coherent, intentional, and emotionally alive.

Senior Cinematographer

Rohan Sharma

A graduate of the Film and Television Institute of India, Rohan brings a cinematographer's rigour to every wedding film. His aerial sequences and slow-motion ceremony footage have become the signature of StoryBookOpulence productions. He is equally at home on a desert dune or a palace rooftop.
