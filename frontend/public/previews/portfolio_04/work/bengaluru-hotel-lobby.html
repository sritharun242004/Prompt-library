The Bengaluru Club — Lobby Renewal

ClientThe Bengaluru Club
DisciplineInteriors
CategoryHotels
LocationBengaluru, Karnataka
Year2023
Area1,800 sqm
Synopsis

A comprehensive interior renewal of the lobby, lounge, and bar of the 136-year-old Bengaluru Club — one of India's oldest and most distinguished private member clubs. The 1,800 sqm project navigates a careful balance: honouring the club's deep institutional memory while making it relevant to a new generation of members. The lobby's double-height colonial space had accumulated decades of incremental changes — a false ceiling here, a tile update there — that obscured its original character. Our first act was an act of subtraction: removing these accretions to reveal the original proportions, which proved to be exceptionally fine. The restored space was then refurnished with a collection of bespoke pieces that combine the comfort of a private home with the gravitas appropriate to the institution.

Approach

The design language is deliberately eclectic in the manner of a house that has been lived in for over a century — nothing matches perfectly, yet everything belongs together. Custom joinery in aged teak references the club's colonial furniture collection while being clearly contemporary in its detailing. A new bar counter in polished black granite with solid brass fittings anchors the lounge end of the space. The lighting strategy uses a layered system of pendant lights, wall sconces, and concealed uplighting to create an atmosphere that transitions from the bright welcome of a working lobby to the warm intimacy of a late-evening bar without any physical reconfiguration. Archival photographs from the club's 136-year history were curated and framed as a sequential installation along the main corridor, creating a timeline that connects arriving members to the institution's remarkable past.

Materials & Finishes

  • Aged teak joinery
  • Polished black granite
  • Solid brass fittings
  • Hand-knotted wool carpet
  • Antique mirror panels
