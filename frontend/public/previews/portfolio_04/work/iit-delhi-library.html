Central Knowledge Hub

ClientIndian Institute of Technology Delhi
DisciplineArchitecture
CategoryInstitutional
LocationNew Delhi, Delhi
Year2022
Area12,000 sqm
Synopsis

A 12,000 sqm central library conceived as a "knowledge landscape" — a topography of reading terraces, breakout alcoves, and digital collaboration zones under a single undulating roof. The building serves as the intellectual heart of a campus of 8,000 students, providing a range of study environments from silent individual reading rooms to large-format collaborative halls. A continuous section ramp connects all levels without interruption, allowing the entire collection — physical and digital — to be navigated in a single unbroken journey. The roof is the building's defining gesture: a parametric shell of ribbed precast concrete panels, each tilted to capture northern light and exclude direct solar gain. At night, the glowing underbelly of the roof becomes a landmark visible across the campus.

Approach

The roof structure is a parametric shell of ribbed precast concrete, engineered to provide north-facing daylighting to every study level. Levels are connected by a continuous ramp — a scholar's promenade that makes the entire collection navigable on foot. The structural engineering team modelled over 300 panel configurations before arriving at the final geometry, which achieves a 40% reduction in solar heat gain compared to a conventional flat roof. Compressed earth block walls form the primary enclosure at ground level — a material chosen for its thermal mass and low embodied carbon, and sourced from a manufacturer 80km from the site. The building targets a GRIHA 5-star rating. A carefully calibrated acoustic strategy ensures that even the most open collaborative zones maintain a library-appropriate sound environment through material absorption and room geometry.
