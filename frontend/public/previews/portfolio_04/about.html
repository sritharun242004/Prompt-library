Studio

Studio Lotus was founded in New Delhi in 2002 by Ambrish Arora, Ankur Choksi, and Sidhartha Talwar. We are an interdisciplinary practice working across architecture, interiors, and adaptive reuse — with a shared commitment to contextual design rooted in place, craft, and material honesty.

Our work spans boutique hospitality, institutional buildings, cultural institutions, and private residences across the Indian subcontinent. Each project begins with close listening — to the land, the client, and the local making traditions that give a place its character.

We believe architecture is most powerful when it is quietly embedded in its context — when it amplifies what is already extraordinary about a place rather than competing with it. This philosophy guides every decision, from urban scale to the choice of a doorknob.

How We Work

  1. 01Research

    Every project begins with deep immersion in place, context, and people. We conduct site studies, archival research, material surveys, and sustained conversations with clients, users, and local communities. This phase typically takes 8–12 weeks and is never rushed — the quality of what follows depends entirely on the quality of what is learned here.

  2. 02Design

    Design emerges from research rather than preceding it. We work iteratively across scales simultaneously — urban, building, room, detail — understanding that decisions at each scale are contingent on all the others. We present design work in physical models and large-format drawings, never relying solely on renders. Our clients make better decisions when they can feel the space before it is built.

  3. 03Delivery

    We maintain a dedicated project delivery team that manages contractor relationships, site supervision, and quality control throughout construction. Senior architects visit every active site weekly. We use a rigorous drawing-issue protocol that ensures contractors always work from the most current documentation. Mock-ups of all critical material junctions are approved on site before full-scale execution begins.

  4. 04Documentation

    At project completion, we produce a comprehensive archive: measured drawings of the built work, a material specification record, a photographic survey, and a written project narrative. This documentation is provided to the client and retained in our studio archive. We believe the record of how a building was made is as valuable as the building itself — a resource for future custodians and a contribution to architectural knowledge.
