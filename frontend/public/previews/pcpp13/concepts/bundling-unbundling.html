Bundling and Unbundling

The history of media and software is a repeating cycle: incumbents bundle services to lower per-unit cost; new entrants unbundle to serve specific needs better; successful unbundlers accumulate features and re-bundle at a higher level of abstraction.

Jim Barksdale, former CEO of Netscape, summarised the two ways to make money in business: bundling and unbundling. The internet has made both faster and more brutal.

Bundling logic: When a supplier can produce multiple goods or services and offer them together at a lower combined price than buying each separately, consumers benefit and the supplier gains scale and switching-cost advantages. Cable television was the classic bundle: 200 channels for $80/month, when individual channels would have cost more in total if purchased separately.

Unbundling logic: New entrants identify the most valuable component of a bundle, extract it, and offer it at lower price or higher quality than the incumbent bundle can match. Netflix unbundled premium movies from cable. Spotify unbundled music. LinkedIn unbundled job listings from newspapers.

The re-bundling cycle: Successful unbundlers grow their user base, accumulate adjacent features, and eventually become bundlers themselves — at which point they become targets for the next generation of unbundlers.

The media case study: Newspapers bundled news, classifieds, sports, weather, and entertainment. Craigslist unbundled classifieds. Google unbundled news search. Monster/LinkedIn unbundled job listings. Sports apps unbundled sports scores. In each case, the unbundler captured the highest-margin, highest-frequency segment of newspaper usage, stranding the bundle economics.

The application to software: Salesforce bundled CRM features that were previously spread across contact managers and spreadsheets. Competitors are now unbundling specific Salesforce functions (revenue intelligence, customer success management, contract management) and offering them at higher quality for lower cost.

Applied Examples

  • Craigslist unbundled classifieds from newspapers, then failed to re-bundle
  • Spotify unbundled music from iTunes, then re-bundled with podcasts
  • Slack unbundled email for team coordination, now adding more features
  • HubSpot re-bundling marketing tools that were previously separate SaaS products
