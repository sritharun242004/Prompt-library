The Analyst

James Calder

Founder and Principal Analyst

James Calder spent twelve years as a strategy consultant at a global firm, advising technology companies and investors on competitive positioning, market entry, and M&A. Before consulting, he worked in product at a payments company and in investment banking covering media and telecommunications.

He founded AnalystAusterity in 2018 because he could not find the kind of analysis he wanted to read: rigorous, framework-driven, independent, and free of the conflicts that distort most media coverage of technology businesses.

He holds degrees in economics and mathematics. He lives in London.

Methodology

Every analysis at AnalystAusterity begins with a question about competitive dynamics or business structure — not with a news event. The goal is not to explain what happened but to understand why it happened and what it implies for the strategic landscape.

The primary analytical frameworks — Aggregation Theory, the Smiling Curve, Two-Sided Markets, Jobs to be Done, Modular vs Integrated — are applied consistently across analyses to build a coherent, cumulative body of work rather than a series of disconnected takes.

Sources are public: company filings, earnings calls, regulatory submissions, academic research, and interviews. No analysis relies on anonymous sources or unpublished information. When we change our view, we say so.

Editorial principles

  • No advertising. No sponsored content. No affiliate links. Revenue comes entirely from subscriptions.
  • No equity positions in companies covered. No advisory relationships with covered companies without disclosure.
  • Corrections are issued promptly and prominently when we are wrong.
  • Subscriber data is not sold or shared. The relationship is between the publication and the reader, with no intermediary.
