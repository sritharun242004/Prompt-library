About

The story behind the lens

We are a two-person photography practice based between Mumbai and Bengaluru, dedicated to the art of documenting love in the world's most extraordinary places.

AeroPerspective began in 2014 with a single conviction: that destination weddings deserve photographers who are as invested in the destination as they are in the couple. Over ten years, we have built a way of working that is as much about deep preparation and location knowledge as it is about the intuition of the moment.

We shoot primarily on Fujifilm medium-format digital and analogue film. We believe in the material quality of the photographic image — its weight, its grain, its relationship to light. Our editing is restrained and timeless; we resist trends, because the photographs we make today will be looked at for fifty years.

“The best destination wedding photograph is one that could only have been made in that place, with those people, at that exact moment.”

— Aakash Mehta, Co-Founder

The Team

Aakash Mehta

Lead Photographer & Creative Director

Aakash has spent the last decade photographing weddings and landscapes across four continents. Born in Ahmedabad and trained at the National Institute of Design, his work sits at the intersection of documentary storytelling and fine-art photography. He shoots on Fujifilm medium format and is known for his ability to find stillness in the most celebratory of moments.

SpecialityLandscape & ceremony

Priya Nair

Second Photographer & Film Lead

Priya grew up between Kochi and London, and her photography reflects that duality — rooted in the textures of South India but drawn to the graphic quality of European light. She specialises in portraits and candid documentary work, and manages all analogue and film photography for AeroPerspective.

SpecialityPortraits & film

Our Approach

Preparation

Before every project, we research the destination exhaustively. We study the light at different times of year, map the locations, understand the architecture and landscape. By the time we arrive, the destination is not new to us.

Documentary instinct

We are observers first. We position ourselves to witness what is happening, not to direct it. The moments we are most proud of are the ones that would have existed even if we were not there.

Fine-art finishing

Every image is individually graded in post-production. We do not apply batch presets. Our finishing is guided by the light of each specific day and the emotional register of the story being told.
