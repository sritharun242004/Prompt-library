The Craft of Indigo

5 December 2024·7 min read

Indigo does not hurry. Each dip in the vat deepens the colour by a shade — and a jacket dyed by hand in Kutch carries that patience into every room it enters.

The vat smells ancient. It ferments — lye, madder, time — and the fabric enters pale and comes out a shade darker than before. Indigo requires repetition. You cannot hurry blue.

The artisans in Kutch have been doing this for generations. The Nilgiri Indigo Jacket took four days of dipping and drying before it reached the shade we were looking for: deep, uneven, honest.

Every garment dyed this way is different. Every jacket holds the specific weather of those four days. That is not imperfection. That is the whole point.
