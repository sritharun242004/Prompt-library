Malabar in Bloom

14 February 2025·4 min read

Block prints that carry centuries of craft memory. Ceramic bowls shaped by Kutch hands. The Malabar collection is for those who wear their heritage lightly.

The block-printer in Bagru begins before sunrise. By the time the town wakes, he has already transferred a dozen motifs — peacock, lotus, vine — onto fabric that will travel farther than he ever has.

We watched him work for an hour without speaking. The repetition was not monotonous. It was meditative. Each press of the carved wood block was a small act of remembering.

The Malabar Block Print Dress holds that memory. Wear it to dinner, wear it to a market — the print holds its meaning regardless. Heritage does not require ceremony.
