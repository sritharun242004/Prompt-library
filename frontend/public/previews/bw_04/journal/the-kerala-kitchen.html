The Kerala Kitchen

28 March 2025·6 min read

What does it mean to cook with intention? From bamboo trays to khadi tablecloths, the Kerala collection brings the spirit of a monsoon kitchen to every table.

The kitchen in a Kerala home is not a room — it is a philosophy. Steam, spice, the sound of copper hitting stone. We spent three days with a family in Thrissur who cook every meal from memory, without a single written recipe.

The bamboo tray appeared each morning loaded with coffee and something fried in coconut oil. By the time we left, we had adopted the tray as our own ritual object — a surface on which to arrange the morning before beginning it.

Slow cooking, like slow fashion, is an act of refusal. It refuses the shortcut. It refuses the disposable. It insists that things made by hand, used with care, carry a different kind of weight.
