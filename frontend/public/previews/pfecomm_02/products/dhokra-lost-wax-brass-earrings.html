The Craft Story

Made in Bankura, West Bengal

Dhokra metal casting is one of the earliest non-ferrous metal casting techniques, first used in the Indus Valley Civilisation. The tribal communities of Bastar, Chhattisgarh, and Bankura, West Bengal are the primary practitioners today. The process has remained virtually unchanged for 4,000 years.

This piece was made by fairly paid artisans who own their tools and work in their own workshops or homes. Warm Craft pays above market price and guarantees at least 30 days lead time — no rush orders that compromise quality.

Meet the Artisan

Sukra Mahato

Bankura, West Bengal·24 years experience

Sukra comes from the Dhokra artisan community and has won the State Craft Award for his intricate tribal motif designs. He is teaching the technique to 12 young apprentices.
