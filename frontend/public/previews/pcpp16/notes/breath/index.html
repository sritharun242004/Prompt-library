Breath

by James Nestor

8/10Read January 2024

Top 3 Takeaways

  • 1.Nasal breathing is fundamentally different from mouth breathing — the nose filters, humidifies, and adds nitric oxide that improves oxygen uptake by 18%
  • 2.The modern human skull has changed more in the last 300 years than in the previous 300,000 — our mouths are too small for our teeth, crowding our airways
  • 3.Slow breathing at around 5-6 breaths per minute (resonance breathing) maximizes heart rate variability and has therapeutic effects on anxiety and asthma

Highlights & Reflections

This note captures the most durable ideas from Breath by James Nestor. The takeaways above represent the highest-leverage insights — the ideas that changed how I think about a problem, not just what I know about it.

The best books don't just inform — they restructure the mental models you use to understand reality. I read this one at a time when the category of biohacking was particularly relevant to my work, and several of its frameworks have become part of my regular thinking vocabulary.

Rating: 8/10. I give high ratings to books that I find myself citing in conversations and returning to in notes months after reading. This one qualifies on both counts.
