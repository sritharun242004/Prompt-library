Coronary Artery Disease

Cardiology

Overview

Coronary artery disease (CAD) results from atherosclerotic plaque build-up in the coronary arteries, reducing blood flow to the heart muscle. It is the most common cause of heart attack and a leading driver of heart failure. Our comprehensive CAD programme integrates advanced imaging, intervention, and prevention.

Signs & Symptoms

  • Stable angina (chest tightness with exertion)
  • Unstable angina at rest
  • Acute myocardial infarction (heart attack)
  • Shortness of breath
  • Fatigue
  • Heart failure symptoms in advanced disease

This list is not exhaustive. If you are experiencing symptoms, consult a qualified physician.

Treatments at CareCompass

  • ✓Optimal medical therapy (statins, antiplatelet agents, beta-blockers)
  • ✓Percutaneous coronary intervention (PCI/stenting)
  • ✓Coronary artery bypass grafting (CABG)
  • ✓Cardiac rehabilitation
  • ✓Lifestyle modification programme
  • ✓Intravascular imaging-guided procedures
