Harissa Prawns

30 min total
Beginner
Serves 2
4.5
Chef Daniel Kim

Jumbo tiger prawns marinated in house-made rose harissa with preserved lemon and smoked paprika, grilled over high heat, and served over a cloud of hummus with pomegranate and dukkah. North Africa meets the Mediterranean grill.

Prep

20 min

Cook

10 min

Total

30 min

Mise en Place

500 g Jumbo tiger prawns

shell-on, deveined, butterfly cut

3 tbsp Rose harissa paste

1/2 piece Preserved lemon

rind only, finely chopped

1 tsp Smoked paprika

3 tbsp Extra virgin olive oil

200 g Smooth hummus

store-bought or homemade

50 g Pomegranate arils

2 tbsp Dukkah

1/4 cup Fresh mint

leaves only

1 lemon Lemon

juice only, to finish

Method

  1. 1

    Marinate the prawns

    Combine prawns with harissa, preserved lemon, paprika, and olive oil. Toss to coat thoroughly. Marinate 15–30 minutes (no longer — the acid begins to cure the protein).

    20 min

  2. 2

    Grill the prawns

    Heat a grill or grill pan to its maximum temperature. Grill prawns shell-side down for 2 minutes, then turn and cook flesh-side down for 1 minute. They are done when just opaque and slightly charred.

    Chef's Note

    Prawns are done before you think they are. Trust the curl — a C-shape is cooked; an O-shape is overcooked.

    4 min

  3. 3

    Build the plate

    Spread hummus across a wide plate. Arrange grilled prawns on top. Scatter pomegranate arils, dukkah, and fresh mint. Finish with a generous squeeze of lemon.

    3 min

Chef's Kitchen Notes

1.

Rose harissa has a more floral, complex heat than regular harissa. If you can only find regular harissa, add a pinch of dried rose petals.

2.

The shell on the prawns is a flavour carrier. Encourage guests to suck the shells.

3.

This dish is also excellent with flatbread. The harissa-hummus combination is pure pleasure.

Chef's Tip

Pro Kitchen Insight

“The single most important moment in cooking prawns is knowing when to stop. They continue cooking after they leave the heat. Pull them at C-shape and rest for 30 seconds — they will be perfect.”

— Chef Daniel Kim
