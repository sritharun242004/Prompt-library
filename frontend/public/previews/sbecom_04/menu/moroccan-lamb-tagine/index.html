Moroccan Lamb Tagine

3h total
Intermediate
Serves 4
4.9
Chef Daniel Kim

Shoulder of lamb slow-braised with ras el hanout, preserved lemon, and Medjool dates until the meat is falling-tender and the sauce is perfumed with the ancient spice routes of North Africa. Served over hand-rolled couscous.

Prep

30 min

Cook

2h 30min

Total

3h

Mise en Place

1.2 kg Lamb shoulder

cut into large chunks

2 tbsp Ras el hanout

1 whole Preserved lemon

pulp discarded, rind finely sliced

8 dates Medjool dates

pitted, halved

2 large White onions

grated

5 cloves Garlic

minced

3 cm Fresh ginger

finely grated

1/2 tsp Saffron

steeped in 3 tbsp warm water

400 ml Lamb stock

400 g Canned chickpeas

drained and rinsed

1/3 cup Fresh coriander

leaves only

50 g Toasted flaked almonds

Method

  1. 1

    Marinate the lamb

    Toss lamb in ras el hanout, half the grated onion, garlic, and ginger. Refrigerate 4 hours or overnight.

    10 min

  2. 2

    Sear in the tagine or Dutch oven

    Brown marinated lamb in argan oil or olive oil in batches. Do not crowd the pan. Remove and set aside.

    20 min

  3. 3

    Build the base

    Sauté remaining onion until golden. Add saffron water, stock, and preserved lemon rind. Return lamb. Cover tightly and braise at 150°C (300°F) for 2 hours.

    2h 10min

  4. 4

    Add dates and chickpeas

    Add dates and chickpeas in the final 30 minutes. The dates should almost dissolve into the sauce, providing sweetness and body.

    30 min

  5. 5

    Finish and serve

    Taste and adjust with salt and preserved lemon. Serve over couscous, scattered with fresh coriander and toasted almonds.

    5 min

Chef's Kitchen Notes

1.

Ras el hanout quality varies enormously. Buy from a North African spice shop if possible.

2.

Preserved lemon is a flavour completely unlike fresh lemon — it provides a fermented, complex salinity. Do not substitute.

3.

Harissa on the side for those who want heat — never add it to the tagine itself.

Step-by-Step Guide

How to build a spice foundation that is complex, aromatic, and perfectly balanced.

  1. 1

    Toast whole spices in a dry pan before grinding — 90 seconds over medium heat until fragrant.

  2. 2

    Bloom ground spices in oil or ghee at the start of cooking to awaken fat-soluble compounds.

  3. 3

    Use ras el hanout as a foundation, then layer individual spices for depth.

  4. 4

    Balance warm spices (cumin, coriander) with sweet (cinnamon, allspice) and sharp (ginger).

  5. 5

    Add preserved lemon only in the final 15 minutes — it provides brightness, not background.

  6. 6

    Harissa is a finishing element, not a cooking base — stir in at the end.
