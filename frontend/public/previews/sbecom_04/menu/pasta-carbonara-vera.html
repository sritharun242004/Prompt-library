ItalianChef's Pick

Pasta Carbonara Vera

30 min total
Intermediate
Serves 2
4.8
Chef Elena Rossi

Roman carbonara as it was and always will be — no cream, no shortcuts, no substitutions. Guanciale-rendered fat, eggs, Pecorino Romano, and Parmigiano-Reggiano transform into a sauce of silk and gold. The technique is the recipe.

Prep

10 min

Cook

20 min

Total

30 min

Mise en Place

200 g Rigatoni or spaghetti

bronze-die extruded

120 g Guanciale

cut into lardons

4 large Egg yolks

room temperature

40 g Pecorino Romano

finely grated

40 g Parmigiano-Reggiano

finely grated

2 tsp Black pepper

coarsely ground, toasted

200 ml Pasta cooking water

reserved and boiling

Method

  1. 1

    Render the guanciale

    Render guanciale in a cold pan, increasing heat gradually to medium. Cook until edges are crispy and fat is rendered — 8 minutes. Remove pan from heat.

    Chef's Note

    Guanciale is not pancetta. Its fat content and flavour are different and non-substitutable in this context.

    8 min

  2. 2

    Prepare the egg mixture

    Whisk together egg yolks, half the Pecorino, half the Parmigiano-Reggiano, and black pepper until smooth. Add 2 tbsp pasta water to temper.

    3 min

  3. 3

    Cook pasta

    Cook pasta in aggressively salted water, 2 minutes less than package time. Reserve 200ml pasta water. Drain pasta.

    10 min

  4. 4

    Marry pasta and guanciale

    Return pan with guanciale and fat to lowest possible heat. Add hot pasta and toss vigorously for 30 seconds. The pasta must be very hot when the egg hits it.

    2 min

  5. 5

    Emulsify the sauce

    Remove pan entirely from heat. Pour egg mixture over pasta. Toss constantly and quickly, adding pasta water splash by splash until a glossy, creamy sauce coats every strand. Work fast — the residual heat is sufficient.

    Chef's Note

    The only enemy of carbonara is heat. If the pan is too hot when the egg hits, you have scrambled eggs.

    2 min

Chef's Kitchen Notes

1.

The pan must be off the heat when you add the eggs. The residual heat of the pasta and pan is exactly enough.

2.

Add pasta water slowly — you may not need all 200ml. Stop when the sauce coats the pasta like cream.

3.

Serve immediately in warm bowls. Carbonara is the most time-sensitive dish in this collection.

Recipe by

Chef Elena Rossi

Executive Chef & Culinary Director

Trained at Le Cordon Bleu Paris and honed her craft across three-Michelin-star kitchens in Lyon and New York, Elena brings classical French technique to the heart of Italian cooking. Her philosophy: great food begins with honest ingredients. She leads BlueChef's culinary vision and personally designs the French and Italian menus each season.

Chef's Tip

Pro Kitchen Insight

“The ratio for true carbonara: 2 egg yolks per person, 30g each of Pecorino and Parmigiano-Reggiano, 60g guanciale. Memorise this and you will never need a recipe again.”

— Chef Elena Rossi
