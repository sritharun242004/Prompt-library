Bouillabaisse

1h 30min total
Pro Chef
Serves 4
4.8
Chef Elena Rossi

The great Provençal fisherman's stew, elevated to a banquet. Saffron-scented broth, rouille-slathered croutons, and an ocean of sustainably sourced seafood — each element telling the story of the Mediterranean coast.

Prep

45 min

Cook

45 min

Total

1h 30min

Mise en Place

600 g Mixed firm fish fillets

monkfish, sea bass, bream — cut into chunks

12 large Shell-on prawns

400 g Mussels

scrubbed and debearded

1/2 tsp Saffron threads

steeped in 2 tbsp warm water

1 medium Fennel bulb

thinly sliced

2 medium Leeks

white part only, sliced

400 g Plum tomatoes

roughly chopped

200 ml Dry white wine

1 litre Fish stock

4 cloves Garlic

minced

1 strip Orange peel

5 tbsp Extra virgin olive oil

Method

  1. 1

    Build the soffritto

    In a wide, deep pan sweat fennel, leeks, and garlic in olive oil until softened — 10 minutes. Add tomatoes and cook until collapsed.

    15 min

  2. 2

    Add wine and saffron

    Pour in wine, add saffron water, orange peel, and fish stock. Simmer 20 minutes. Season carefully.

    20 min

  3. 3

    Add fish and shellfish

    Add firm fish first. After 3 minutes, add prawns and mussels. Cover and cook until mussels have opened and fish is just cooked through — about 5 minutes more.

    8 min

  4. 4

    Make the rouille

    While stew cooks, blend roasted garlic, saffron, egg yolk, and olive oil into a thick, rust-coloured aioli. Season with cayenne.

    5 min

  5. 5

    Serve

    Ladle stew into warm bowls. Float toasted croutons on top, each crowned with a generous spoonful of rouille. Finish with fresh fennel fronds.

    5 min

Chef's Kitchen Notes

1.

A proper bouillabaisse requires at least three types of fish — variety is structural, not decorative.

2.

The rouille must be made by hand or with a stick blender — never a food processor, which breaks the emulsion.

3.

Do not overcook the seafood. Five minutes from adding is the absolute maximum.

Recipe by

Chef Elena Rossi

Executive Chef & Culinary Director

Trained at Le Cordon Bleu Paris and honed her craft across three-Michelin-star kitchens in Lyon and New York, Elena brings classical French technique to the heart of Italian cooking. Her philosophy: great food begins with honest ingredients. She leads BlueChef's culinary vision and personally designs the French and Italian menus each season.

Chef's Tip

Pro Kitchen Insight

“The entire character of the dish comes from the broth. Taste it before adding seafood and it should already be extraordinary — deeply flavoured, saffron-gold, with a whisper of the sea.”

— Chef Elena Rossi
