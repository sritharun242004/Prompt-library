Pan-Roasted Sea Bass with Beurre Blanc

45 min total
Pro Chef
Serves 2
4.7
Chef Elena Rossi

Crispy-skinned Mediterranean sea bass over a bright, buttery beurre blanc with capers, tarragon, and a fennel confit. The technique of pressing the skin is all that stands between a good cook and a great one.

Prep

20 min

Cook

25 min

Total

45 min

Mise en Place

2 fillets Sea bass fillets (skin on)

6 oz each, scaled

100 ml Dry white wine

2 tbsp White wine vinegar

2 small Shallots

finely minced

120 g Cold unsalted butter

cut into 1cm cubes

1 small Fennel bulb

thinly sliced

1 tbsp Capers

rinsed

1 tbsp Fresh tarragon

leaves only

1 lemon Lemon

juice only

Method

  1. 1

    Confit the fennel

    In a small saucepan, combine fennel slices with olive oil to barely cover. Cook over very low heat (180°F/80°C) for 25 minutes until silky and tender.

    25 min

  2. 2

    Prepare the beurre blanc base

    Combine wine, vinegar, and shallots. Reduce over medium heat until nearly dry — about 2 tablespoons of liquid remain.

    8 min

  3. 3

    Sear the sea bass

    Score the skin with 3 shallow cuts. Heat a non-stick pan until shimmering. Place fish skin-side down and press gently for the first 30 seconds. Cook 70% on the skin — about 5 minutes — then flip for 30 seconds only.

    Chef's Note

    The skin carries all the flavour. Protect it — only flip once.

    6 min

  4. 4

    Mount the beurre blanc

    Return the reduction pan to the lowest possible heat. Add cold butter two cubes at a time, whisking constantly. Finish with capers, tarragon, and a squeeze of lemon.

    Chef's Note

    If the sauce breaks, rescue by whisking in a tablespoon of cold water.

    5 min

Chef's Kitchen Notes

1.

The secret to crispy fish skin: bone dry before it hits the pan.

2.

Beurre blanc cannot be reheated. Make it immediately before service.

3.

Cold butter, added slowly, is the only way to build the emulsion.

Step-by-Step Guide

Transform fond and butter into a glossy, restaurant-quality sauce in under five minutes.

  1. 1

    After searing, remove protein and pour off excess fat, leaving browned fond on the pan.

  2. 2

    Deglaze with wine or stock while heat is still high — scrape all the fond.

  3. 3

    Reduce liquid by half over medium-high heat.

  4. 4

    Lower heat to a gentle simmer and add cold butter, one tablespoon at a time.

  5. 5

    Swirl or whisk constantly — never let it boil once butter is added.

  6. 6

    Season, pass through a fine sieve if desired, and serve immediately.
