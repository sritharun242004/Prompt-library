Thai Massaman Curry

1h 20min total
Intermediate
Serves 4
4.8
Chef Daniel Kim

The Persian-influenced curry of the Thai royal court — deeply complex, warmly spiced with cardamom, clove, and cinnamon, with a richness from coconut milk and peanuts that places it in a category entirely its own. Considered Thailand's most complex curry.

Prep

25 min

Cook

55 min

Total

1h 20min

Mise en Place

800 g Beef brisket

cut into 4cm cubes

4 tbsp Massaman curry paste

best quality, preferably Mae Ploy

800 ml Coconut milk

2 cans, full fat

400 g Waxy potatoes

quartered

80 g Roasted peanuts

150 g Pearl onions or shallots

peeled

2 tbsp Palm sugar

2 tbsp Fish sauce

1 tbsp Tamarind paste

4 pods Cardamom pods

lightly crushed

1 stick Cinnamon stick

4 leaves Kaffir lime leaves

torn

Method

  1. 1

    Crack the coconut cream

    Open the coconut milk cans without shaking. Scoop the thick cream from the top (about 200ml) into a wide pan. Cook over medium heat until the cream separates and the oil rises — 8 minutes. The cream will bubble and fry the paste.

    Chef's Note

    This step — called "cracking" — is the foundation of all Thai curry. Do not skip it.

    10 min

  2. 2

    Fry the curry paste

    Add massaman paste to the cracked cream. Fry, stirring constantly, for 3 minutes until intensely fragrant and the oil turns orange. Add cardamom, cinnamon, and lime leaves.

    5 min

  3. 3

    Add beef and remaining coconut milk

    Add beef and toss to coat in the paste. Add remaining coconut milk, palm sugar, fish sauce, and tamarind. Bring to a gentle simmer. Cover and cook for 35 minutes.

    40 min

  4. 4

    Add potatoes, onions, and peanuts

    Add potatoes, pearl onions, and peanuts. Continue simmering, uncovered, for 15 minutes until potatoes are tender and sauce has thickened slightly.

    15 min

  5. 5

    Balance and serve

    Taste and adjust the four flavours: sweet (palm sugar), sour (tamarind), salty (fish sauce), and spicy (curry paste). Serve with jasmine rice and crispy shallots.

    5 min

Chef's Kitchen Notes

1.

Quality of the curry paste is everything. Mae Ploy is the standard; Maesri is the budget option. Never use generic supermarket paste.

2.

Massaman is a slow-heat curry. Never allow it to boil hard — a vigorous bubble breaks the coconut milk.

3.

The final balance of four flavours (sweet, sour, salty, spicy) is what separates a good massaman from a great one. Taste and adjust generously.

Chef's Tip

Pro Kitchen Insight

“Massaman is one of the very few Thai curries that actually improves significantly on day two. The spices continue to bloom overnight in the refrigerator. If you can, make it a day ahead.”

— Chef Daniel Kim
