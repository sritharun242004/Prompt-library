Tarte Tatin

1h 5min total
Intermediate
Serves 4
4.9
Chef Elena Rossi

The upside-down apple tart from Sologne that has conquered the world's finest pastry counters. Buttery, caramelised apples set atop a crackling puff pastry shell, inverted at the table in a moment of theatrical drama.

Prep

25 min

Cook

40 min

Total

1h 5min

Mise en Place

6 large Golden Delicious apples

peeled, cored, halved

80 g Unsalted butter

150 g Caster sugar

250 g All-butter puff pastry

rolled to fit your skillet

1 pod Vanilla pod

seeds scraped

2 tbsp Calvados

optional but recommended

1 pinch Sea salt flakes

200 ml Crème fraîche

to serve

Method

  1. 1

    Make the caramel

    In an oven-proof skillet (24cm), melt butter and sugar over medium heat. Do not stir — swirl. Cook until a deep amber caramel develops, about 12 minutes.

    Chef's Note

    You want the caramel very dark — almost burnt. Timid caramel makes a timid tarte.

    12 min

  2. 2

    Arrange apples

    Remove from heat. Add vanilla seeds and Calvados. Arrange apple halves cut-side up in the caramel, packed tightly. They will shrink.

    8 min

  3. 3

    Bake apples

    Return to medium heat on the hob for 5 minutes until apples begin to soften and release juice. Then transfer to 190°C (375°F) oven for 20 minutes.

    25 min

  4. 4

    Top with pastry and bake

    Remove from oven. Drape pastry over apples, tucking edges inside the skillet. Return to 200°C (400°F) for 20 minutes until pastry is deep golden and crisp.

    20 min

  5. 5

    Rest and invert

    Rest 10 minutes. Place a plate over the pan and invert decisively in one motion. Do not hesitate — hesitation causes spillage. Scrape any remaining caramel over the apples.

    Chef's Note

    The 10-minute rest lets the caramel firm slightly so the tarte holds its shape on inversion.

    10 min

Chef's Kitchen Notes

1.

Use only Golden Delicious or Granny Smith — they hold their shape. Never use cooking apples that disintegrate.

2.

The caramel should smell almost burnt. If it smells sweet and pleasant, it is not dark enough.

3.

Serve within 20 minutes of inverting. Tarte tatin does not wait.

Recipe by

Chef Elena Rossi

Executive Chef & Culinary Director

Trained at Le Cordon Bleu Paris and honed her craft across three-Michelin-star kitchens in Lyon and New York, Elena brings classical French technique to the heart of Italian cooking. Her philosophy: great food begins with honest ingredients. She leads BlueChef's culinary vision and personally designs the French and Italian menus each season.

Chef's Tip

Pro Kitchen Insight

“The inversion is the only moment that separates a successful tarte tatin from a disaster. Practice with a cold skillet once before making the real thing — it builds the muscle memory for a confident, clean flip.”

— Chef Elena Rossi
