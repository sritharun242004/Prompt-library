Motor Technology

How the Vortex V12 motor system works.

01
Air Intake

Air enters at 120,000 RPM impeller velocity.

The brushless digital motor accelerates incoming air using a titanium impeller spinning at up to 120,000 RPM — generating a negative pressure differential of 18kPa at the inlet. Air is drawn through a precision-machined inlet manifold at 28 litres per second.

18kPa
Inlet negative pressure
02
Cyclonic Separation

14-cone cyclone array separates particles at 500G.

Turbulent airflow is routed into 14 parallel micro-cyclones, each generating up to 500G of centrifugal force. Particles from 10 microns down are flung outward by inertia and deposited into the collection bin. Air exits clean — before reaching the HEPA filter.

500G
Centrifugal separation force
03
HEPA Filtration

HEPA H13 captures residual 0.3-micron particles.

Post-cyclone air passes through a borosilicate glass-fibre HEPA H13 filter with ≤0.05μm mean pore size. The filter captures 99.97% of particles at the most penetrating particle size (MPPS). Exhaust air is certified cleaner than the ambient laboratory air during testing.

99.97%
Particle capture at 0.3μm
