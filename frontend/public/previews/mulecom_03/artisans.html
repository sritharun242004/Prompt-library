Madhubani Painting

Savitri Devi Jha is a third-generation Madhubani artist from Mithila, Bihar. Her paintings depict the cosmology of Maithil women — tree of life, fish motifs, wedding ceremonies — using natural pigments on handmade cotton cloth. Each piece is painted freehand with twigs and fingers.

Dhokra Craft Studio

⭐ Star

Ramu Shilpkar

Chhattisgarh·4.8

Dhokra Craft

Ramu Shilpkar leads a collective of Dhokra craftspeople in Bastar, Chhattisgarh. Using the ancient lost-wax casting technique, they create tribal figurines, jewellery, and lamps in brass. The process has remained unchanged for 4,000 years — beeswax model, clay mould, molten brass pour.

Pashmina Palace

⭐ Star

Ghulam Nabi Wani

Kashmir·5

Pashmina Weaving

Ghulam Nabi Wani's family has woven Pashmina shawls in Srinagar for six generations. The wool comes from Changthangi goats at 14,000 feet altitude in Ladakh. Every shawl is hand-spun on a wooden charkha and woven on a handloom — no machines touch the fiber.

Blue Pottery Jaipur

Kaluram Prajapat

Rajasthan·4.7

Blue Pottery

Kaluram Prajapat is a master potter whose family carries the Persian-influenced Blue Pottery tradition of Jaipur. The clay-free "paste" made from quartz stone, powdered glass, Multani mitti and katira gum is hand-shaped, painted with cobalt oxide, and kiln-fired at low temperatures to produce the distinctive translucent blue glaze.

Since 2001·1,870 sales·4 products

Warli Art House

⭐ Star

Jivya Soma Mashe (Estate)

Maharashtra·4.9

Warli Art

This studio continues the legacy of the late Jivya Soma Mashe, the Padma Shri-awarded artist who brought Warli painting from ceremonial walls of Palghar villages to museum collections worldwide. Today his students and family create authentic Warli works using white rice paste on ochre-washed cloth, with circular compositions celebrating community life.

Bamboo & Cane Crafts

Mrinmoy Nath

Assam·4.8

Bamboo & Cane

Mrinmoy Nath crafts heirloom-quality bamboo and cane products from his workshop in Sualkuchi, Assam. He selects mature muli bamboo from sustainable forest patches, seasoning it for 60 days before weaving. His baskets, trays, and lamp shades have been exhibited at the National Crafts Museum in Delhi.
