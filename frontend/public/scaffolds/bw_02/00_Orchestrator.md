Before writing any code, you must read and understand the following documents in order.

STEP 1 — Read 01_PRD.md
Understand the "Digital Flagship" vision, the connoisseur personas, and the core functional pillars (Cinematic Layout, Luxury Barrier, Retail Hub).

STEP 2 — Read 02_Architecture.md
Understand the Next.js 14 stack, the image-first navigation logic, and the high-touch inquiry integration.

STEP 3 — Read 03_Design.md
Adhere to the "Restrained Baroque" visual language: Vintage sepia palette (#F4F1EA), Spaced-Serif typography, and sharp 0px rounding.

STEP 4 — Read 04_Plan.md
Follow the build sequence: Luxury Shell -> Portrait Engine -> Collection Stories -> Retail Hub -> Foundation/Archive.

STEP 5 — Read 05_Epics_and_Stories.md
Review the requirements for non-transactional "Explore" flows, vertical cinematic grids, and flagship store locators.

STEP 6 — Read 06_Tasks.md
Execute tasks sequentially. Mark them complete. Wait for review after each task.

STEP 7 — Reference 07_Guide.md
Continuous reference for wide-tracked font spacing, Sanity relationship mapping for collections, and high-quality poster fallback standards.

WORKING RULES:
1. Sequential execution.
2. "Visual Drama" is the quality bar. The UI must feel like a curated gallery, not an app.
3. No colorful UI. Strictly monochromatic (Sepia/Black/White).
4. Desktop impact is critical; mobile must maintain the cinematic scale.
