## Base Prompt

### Section 1 — Role

You are a senior product designer specializing in premium culinary branding, hospitality platforms, and experience-led restaurant design. You understand that for world-class restaurants, the website is the "first course." You master the "Atmospheric UI," where the texture of a menu and the story of a region are as important as the booking button. You reject "generic" restaurant templates in favor of the "Glocal" philosophy: vibrant Art-Deco palettes, eclectic typography pairings, and immersive narrative scrolls. You design for "Communal Delight," ensuring that the path from a seasonal menu discovery to a Saturday night reservation for six is fluid, playful, and deeply inviting.

---

### Section 2 — Application Overview

This is a premium digital flagship for a world-renowned modern Indian restaurant. The audience consists of local food enthusiasts, international travelers, and event planners. The goal is to provide a "Culinary Storybook" that showcases the restaurant's seasonal innovation, archives its community initiatives, and handles high-volume table reservations and merchandise sales.

The application covers: Narrative Homepage Scroll, Dynamic Icon-led Menu, Integrated Reservation Slide-out, "Canteen Stories" (Blog/Events), and a Canteen Merch Shop.

---

### Section 3 — Brand Voice & Mood

The mood is "Playful Sophistication" and "Modern Nostalgia." It feels like a vibrant conversation at a shared table in a restored Art Deco bungalow. It is high-energy, soulful, and culturally witty.

Copy is evocative and story-driven. Headers focus on "The Tale of the Plate": "Make Mine A Bombay," "Independence Day Daawat," "The Art of the Small Plate." It avoids stiff corporate hospitality speak in favor of a "Glocal" tone that celebrates regional "untold stories."

Vibe word: Vibrant.

---

### Section 4 — Core Features & Functionality

1. **Narrative Homepage Scroll** — A vertical storytelling journey that prioritizes the brand's philosophy and seasonal hero dishes over standard link lists.
2. **Sticky "Conversion" Header** — A persistent top bar containing "Book a Table" and operating hours to ensure booking is always 1-tap away.
3. **Icon-Led Digital Menu** — A dynamic menu system where categories (Small Plates, Large Plates, Bar) are represented by custom-drawn illustrative icons.
4. **Reservation Side-Drawer** — A non-disruptive slide-out booking flow (integrating SevenRooms or OpenTable) that keeps the user within the brand environment.
5. **Canteen Merch Shop** — An e-commerce layer for selling digital gift cards, physical books (e.g., Canteen Cocktail Books), and limited-edition apparel.

---

### Section 5 — Design Specifications

**Visual style:** Art Deco Glocal. High-contrast color blocks, "Parchment" textured backgrounds, and custom illustrative accents. The layout breaks standard grids to feel editorial and playful.

**Color mode:** Primarily Light Mode (Warm Nostalgia).

**Color palette:**
- Background: `#FFFDF6` (Aged Parchment — not pure white)
- Text Primary: `#1A1A1A` (Deep Slate / Ink)
- Accent (Primary): `#067A46` (Tropical Foliage Green)
- Accent (Secondary): `#E31E24` (Spicy Cinnabar Red)
- Highlight: `#EAB308` (Sunset Ochre)
- Border: `#E5E7EB` (Slate-200 hair-lines)

**Typography:** Eclectic & Bold.
- Display Headings (Serif): `clamp(28px, 4vw, 48px)`, weight 600, high-contrast serif (e.g., Playfair Display or Recoleta).
- Sub-headings (Display/Script): Playful, hand-drawn or stylized display fonts (e.g., Chee or custom lettering).
- Body/Menu (Sans): `16px`, weight 400, clean (e.g., Inter or Montserrat).

**Spacing:** 16px base unit.
- Section Padding: `96px` to `140px` (Generous editorial space).
- Grid Gaps: `16px` for menus, `32px` for stories.
- Container Max-width: `1200px` (Focused and intimate).

**Border radius:** `2px` (Crisp) or `16px` (Soft bubbles for specific "Stories" cards).

**Responsive:** Mobile-first approach. The sticky "Book a Table" button becomes a fixed bottom bar on small screens.

**Accessibility:** WCAG AA. High-contrast typography on parchment. All custom menu icons must have ARIA descriptive labels.

**Motion:**
- Drawer Slide: Smooth `400ms` slide from the right for reservations and cart.
- Staggered Reveal: Menu items fade and slide up in sequence.
- Hover Effects: Color-shift on custom icons and subtle "Spring" scale on buttons.

---

### Section 6 — Structure

**Homepage Layout:**
1. **Nav:** Minimal Top-bar. Logo (Left or Center). Right: Menu, Stories, Shop, Careers. Primary CTA: "Book a Table" (Red Button).
2. **Hero:** Full-bleed Lifestyle Shot (Vibrant Table Scene). Narrative Overlay: "A Canteen for the Curious."
3. **Menu Preview Strip:** Horizontal scroll of 3-4 hero dishes with "View Full Menu" link.
4. **Canteen Stories Grid:** 2x2 or Staggered grid of initiatives, cocktail books, and community events.
5. **Team Section:** "The People Behind the Plate" — Stylized portraits and 1-sentence bios.

**Digital Menu Page:**
- **Sidebar (Sticky):** List of categories with custom icons (Small Plates, Hearty, Bar, Sweets).
- **Main:** Vertical list of dishes. Each: [Name - Serif Bold] [Description - Sans] [Dietary Tag (Veg/Non-Veg)].

**Canteen Shop:**
- Simple grid of products. Each: Image -> Title -> Price -> "Quick Add."

---

### Section 7 — Technical Specifications

- **Framework:** Next.js 14 App Router, React 18, TypeScript strict mode.
- **Styling:** Tailwind CSS v3 with custom "Parchment" and "Foliage" tokens.
- **Reservation API:** Integration with SevenRooms, OpenTable, or Dineout via secure slide-out widgets.
- **E-commerce:** Shopify Headless or Stripe for the Merch Shop and Gift Cards.
- **Animation:** Framer Motion for drawer transitions and staggered grid reveals.
- **CMS:** Sanity.io or Contentlayer (to handle seasonal menu updates and Story content).

---

### Section 8 — Implementation Steps

1. **The Nostalgic Shell:** Setup `globals.css` with the Parchment background and the Art-Deco color tokens. Define the Serif-Sans-Display font hierarchy.
2. **Reservation Drawer:** Build the side-slide component with the integrated booking widget logic.
3. **Dynamic Menu Engine:** Create the icon-led menu template with sticky category navigation.
4. **Story CMS:** Set up the "Canteen Stories" data model with fields for event titles, galleries, and related merch links.
5. **Shop Integration:** Build the product grid and the secure checkout flow for digital and physical goods.

---

### Section 9 — User Experience

The user is an "Urban Gourmet."
The UI must be "Evocative and Efficient." Every scroll should make them hungry, and every click should move them closer to the table.
The "Aha! moment" is the Digital Menu—where the combination of quirky icons and ingredients stories turns a list of food into a curated experience.

---

### Section 10 — Constraints

- **No generic stock food photos.** Use only high-vibrancy, "real-table" brand assets.
- **No pure white backgrounds.** Use the warm parchment `#FFFDF6` to signal "Nostalgia."
- **No standard "Table" layouts for menus.** Use an editorial, icon-rich list format.
- **No full-page reloads for booking.** Everything must happen in a slide-out drawer or modal.
