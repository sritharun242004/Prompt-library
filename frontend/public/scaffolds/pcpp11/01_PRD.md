# 01 — Product Requirements Document
## AuthorialInsight Journalist Portfolio · pcpp_platform_11

### 1. Product Vision
"AuthorialInsight" is a digital hallmark for world-class investigative journalists and authors. It moves beyond a simple "list of links" to create a high-authority archive that balances long-form narrative reportage with global literary prestige. The platform prioritizes ease of discovery for commissioning editors and high-impact visual showcasing of published books, providing elite writers with a digital presence that commands intellectual respect.

**Success Metric:** Average "Credential Depth" (percentage of users who view the Awards/Publication strip before contacting) exceeds 50%.

### 2. Personas
**Jonathan — The Commissioning Editor (Client)**
- Needs to verify a journalist's investigative range and previous publication history.
- Needs: A fast, filterable view of articles from major global outlets (NYT, Guardian) and evidence of previous book success.
- Frustration: Portfolio sites that are disorganized or hard to navigate on mobile.

**Mira — The Literary Event Organizer (Client)**
- Looking for authoritative speakers on youth, technology, or politics.
- Needs: A sophisticated bio, a list of prestigious fellowships (MacDowell, PEN), and a professional way to book the author.
- Frustration: Lack of centralized social proof and professional contact methods.

### 3. Core Features
- **Book-Centric Hero Engine:** A high-impact section showcasing the latest book cover with awards and regional "Buy" links.
- **Prestige Publication Strip:** A horizontal row of monochrome logos from global outlets to establish immediate trust.
- **Filterable Article Portfolio:** A high-performance archive organized by Subject (Politics, Youth) and Publication (The Guardian, Granta).
- **Awards & Fellowship Wall:** A dedicated area for establishing authorial credentials through a clean, typographical list.
- **Sophisticated Bio CMS:** A narrative-led "About" page that handles high-end portraiture and professional history.

### 4. User Journeys
1. **The Vetting Path:** Editor lands on home -> sees the prestigious logo strip -> clicks "Portfolio" -> filters by "Politics" -> reviews a series of long-form reports.
2. **The Acquisition Path:** Fan explores the "Books" section -> reads the acclaim for "Dreamers" -> clicks the regional buy link (Amazon/HUP) -> purchases the book.
3. **The Booking Path:** Organizer visits "About" -> reviews the awards wall -> goes to "Contact" -> submits a formal speaking inquiry.

### 5. Non-Goals
- No "Daily Blog" or casual social feed (Focus is on high-stakes investigative work).
- No community comment sections (Focus is on authorial authority).
- No generic "Newsletter Signup" (Unless specifically requested; prefer direct engagement).

### 6. Constraints
- **Design:** Professional Minimalism. Pure white, sharp corners, editorial serif typography.
- **Commerce:** Must handle regional redirect logic for book purchasing (e.g., US vs India).
- **Performance:** Mandatory sub-2s LCP for book covers and portraits.
