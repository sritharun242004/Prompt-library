# 05 — Epics & Stories
## Work Breakdown · pcpp_platform_11

### Epic 1: Immediate Credibility
*As a visitor, I want a homepage that immediately proves the author's global status.*
- **Story 1.1:** View a horizontal strip of prestigious publication logos (NYT, Guardian, etc.).
- **Story 1.2:** See a high-impact hero showcasing the latest book and its awards.
- **Story 1.3:** Experience a clean, "Intellectual" layout that avoids marketing clutter.

### Epic 2: Organized Reportage
*As an editor, I want to find and read previous reporting by subject or outlet.*
- **Story 2.1:** Browse a chronological archive of articles with clear publication markers.
- **Story 2.2:** Filter the portfolio by themes like "Politics" or "Youth Culture."
- **Story 2.3:** Read article excerpts and click through to the original publication instantly.

### Epic 3: Authorial Presence
*As a reader, I want to learn about the author's books and professional journey.*
- **Story 3.1:** View high-resolution book covers with clear "Buy Now" links.
- **Story 3.2:** Read about the author's fellowships and grants in a dedicated awards wall.
- **Story 3.3:** Navigate a sophisticated bio that establishes personal and professional authority.

### Epic 4: Professional Access
*As a client, I want a simple, high-trust way to reach the author for work.*
- **Story 4.1:** Access a clean "Contact" page that respects professional boundaries.
- **Story 4.2:** Provide event or commission details in a high-contrast, structured form.
- **Story 4.3:** Receive a professional, branded confirmation of my inquiry.
