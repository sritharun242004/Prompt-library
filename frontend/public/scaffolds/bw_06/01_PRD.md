# 01 — Product Requirements Document
## CoastalJoy Restaurant Hub · bw_platform_06

### 1. Product Vision
"CoastalJoy" is a digital escape for modern culinary explorers. It moves beyond the "transactional menu" to create a "Digital Bungalow" where Goan-Portuguese heritage and the "Sussegad" lifestyle are celebrated. The platform prioritizes modular discovery and visual storytelling, providing iconic restaurants with a digital presence that matches the "shabby-chic" charm and tropical joy of their physical spaces.

**Success Metric:** 25% of users engage with "Our Story" or "Rocky the Rooster" content before clicking "Order Online" or viewing the "Happy Times" event strip.

### 2. Personas
**Zoya — The Mumbai Cosmopolitan (Client)**
- Lives in a fast-paced city and seeks "Mini-Vacations" through dining and digital content.
- Needs: A breezy way to browse seasonal cocktails, instant access to delivery links, and a sense of "Community Joy."
- Frustration: Corporate-looking restaurant sites that feel clinical and don't provide a visual escape.

**Marco — The Global Heritage Seeker (Audience)**
- Interested in the historical intersection of Portuguese and Indian cultures.
- Needs: To understand the "Why" behind the cuisine and see evidence of authentic craft preservation (e.g., traditional Goan textiles or architecture).
- Frustration: Lack of narrative context and sites that prioritize "Food Photography" over "Environmental Storytelling."

### 3. Core Features
- **Modular Bento Grid:** A responsive homepage system that organizes brand pillars (Menu, Story, Shop) into scannable, artistic blocks.
- **Rocky the Rooster Mascot:** Integrated narrative cues and animations featuring the brand's witty mascot to build emotional affinity.
- **Interactive Text Menus:** High-readability, categorized digital menus that prioritize SEO and mobile-first scannability.
- **Environmental Hero Engine:** Full-bleed banners that showcase the "shabby-chic" interior details and coastal golden light.
- **Hunger Inc. Sister Hub:** A unified footer navigation that bridges the brand with its prestigious sister properties.

### 4. User Journeys
1. **The Tropical Discovery:** Visitor lands on home -> watches the sun-drenched hero -> scrolls through the bento grid -> clicks "Rocky's Favourite Sips" -> browses the cocktail menu.
2. **The Happy Time Join:** User navigates to the "Happy Times" strip -> sees an upcoming Goan feast event -> clicks "Inquire" to book a large group table.
3. **The Quick Order:** Return user arrives on mobile -> clicks "Order Online" block -> chooses between Swiggy/Zomato based on real-time availability.

### 5. Non-Goals
- No "Native Booking" engine (High-touch services preferred via phone/email).
- No community "Member Login" (Maintain the public, open-house bungalow feel).
- No complex "Recipe" database (Maintain the exclusivity of the in-house experience).

### 6. Constraints
- **Design:** Tropical Shabby-Chic. Sunlight cream palette, terracotta accents, sharp 0px corners, high-contrast serif headings.
- **Performance:** Sub-1s initial load for the bento grid shell (Next.js optimization).
- **Data:** Content blocks must be easily swappable via a CMS to support weekly "Happy Times" updates.
